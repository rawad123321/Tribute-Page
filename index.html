Albert Einstein, one of the most influential physicists in history, was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany. Renowned for his groundbreaking contributions to the field of theoretical physics, Einstein is best known for developing the theory of relativity, which transformed our understanding of space, time, and gravity. His iconic equation, E=mc², encapsulates the profound connection between energy and mass. Beyond his scientific achievements, Einstein was a passionate advocate for pacifism and social justice, and his unique appearance and playful demeanor endeared him to the public. Einstein's legacy extends far beyond the realm of physics, making him a symbol of intellectual curiosity, innovation, and the pursuit of knowledge for the betterment of humanity.

Events in Einstein's life.

  • 1879 (March 14): Albert Einstein is born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire.
  • 1895: Einstein applies to the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETH) in Zurich but fails the entrance exam.
  • 1896: Einstein enrolls at Aarau Cantonal School in Switzerland to complete his secondary education.
  • 1900: Einstein graduates from ETH Zurich as a secondary school teacher in physics and mathematics.
  • 1901: Einstein becomes a Swiss citizen.
  • 1902: Einstein takes a job at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern, where he works as a technical expert third class.
  • 1905 (Annus Mirabilis): Einstein publishes four groundbreaking papers, including his theory of special relativity, the photoelectric effect, and the famous equation E=mc².
  • 1907: Einstein publishes a paper on the equivalence of mass and energy, a crucial step toward the development of general relativity.
  • 1915: Einstein presents the field equations of general relativity, providing a new understanding of gravity.
  • 1919: Sir Arthur Eddington's expedition confirms Einstein's predictions about the bending of light by gravity during a total solar eclipse, making Einstein an international celebrity.
  • 1921: Einstein is awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Einstein emigrates to the United States due to the rise of the Nazi regime in Germany.
  • 1939: Einstein signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning of the potential development of atomic weapons by Nazi Germany, which contributes to the initiation of the Manhattan Project.
  • 1940: Einstein becomes a U.S. citizen.
  • 1945: The atomic bombs are dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading to the end of World War II. Einstein becomes an advocate for nuclear disarmament.
  • 1952: Einstein is offered the presidency of Israel but declines.
  • 1955 (April 18): Albert Einstein passes away in Princeton, New Jersey, at the age of 76.

Albert Einstein Private Life.

Albert Einstein was married twice in his lifetime:

  • Mileva Marić: Einstein's first wife was Mileva Marić, whom he married in 1903. Mileva was also a physicist, and they had two sons together, Hans Albert and Eduard. The couple faced personal and professional challenges, and they eventually divorced in 1919.
  • Elsa Einstein:After his divorce from Mileva, Albert Einstein married his first cousin, Elsa Einstein, in 1919. Elsa had two daughters from her previous marriage, Ilse and Margot. Albert and Elsa remained married until her death in 1936.

Albert Einstein's children:

  • Lieserl Einstein:Lieserl was born in early 1902, but very little is known about her. There are historical records suggesting she might have been given up for adoption or died in infancy. Unfortunately, the details of her life remain largely unknown.
  • Hans Albert Einstein: Born on May 14, 1904, Hans Albert was Einstein's first son with his first wife, Mileva Marić. He went on to become a hydraulic engineer and a professor of civil engineering.
  • Eduard "Tete" Einstein:Eduard, also known as "Tete," was born on July 28, 1910. He was Einstein's second son with Mileva. Eduard suffered from mental health issues, and his relationship with his father was complex. Eduard spent much of his life in psychiatric care.

Albert Einstein's last days.

Albert Einstein's last days were marked by declining health. In the final years of his life, Einstein struggled with health issues, including internal bleeding related to the rupture of an abdominal aortic aneurysm. Despite undergoing surgery to repair the aneurysm, his condition continued to deteriorate. In the later stages of his illness, Einstein expressed a wish to die at home, but due to his critical condition, he was admitted to Princeton Hospital. On April 18, 1955, at the age of 76, Albert Einstein passed away at Princeton Hospital. The cause of death was reported as an abdominal aortic aneurysm.
